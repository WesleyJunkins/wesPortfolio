Activities (a closer look)

I was inducted into Shelton's chapter of Phi Theta Kappa as an officer. My role was to lead a research project with our other officers. After completing a course that trained me to conduct academic research, we explored how poor mental health among college students will affect future generations. This investigation led to the creation of a mental health awareness fair on campus, where several organizations from the Tuscaloosa area set up booths and provided crucial information about their services to students and faculty.

I also worked closely with a psychology professor on campus to build upon my previous research. After this, I presented our findings at the Troy University Psychology Conference.

As part of this team, we will develop software that provides a smooth interface between the human brain and drones. The software development process requires research into human-brain attributes, as well as algorithms to compute brain waves.

I also recently joined the HCI research team of a professor at The University of Alabama. In fact, I developed this portfolio while learning Javascript and ThreeJS, which are tools we will use in HCI research.
