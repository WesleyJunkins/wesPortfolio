  • Mental Health among College Students and its Effects on Future Generations

    May, 2021

    As my first introduction to academic research, this project involved a literature review of previous research that looked at the mental health among college students. We added to this by specifically looking at the mental health among community college students and the effect poor mental health had on future generations. Since college-aged adults are generally the ones who will birth the next generation, looking into the effects of their mental health on their children, and their childrens' children, is a worthwhile pursuit. We surveyed students from multiple community colleges in Alabama attaining information about their mental state, their life choices, their home-life, and much more. We used this data to design an on-campus event dedicated to informing students of the resources available to them (since most students replied that they were unaware of the numerous local resources at their disposal). Many local organizations from the community attended the event distributing crucial material on the resources they provided. A final survey of the event attendies showed that most students were introduced to helpful resources they were previously unaware of. These resources ranged from drug abuse aid, food pantries, mental healthcare providers, and other general community-help centers.
